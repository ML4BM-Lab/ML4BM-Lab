About the Group

Principal Investigator

Mikel Hernaez

The ML4BM Group develops computational methods at the intersection of artificial intelligence and biomedical sciences. Our research spans medical imaging, multi-omics data analysis, and interpretable models for clinical decision support.

We are based at the Center for Applied Medical Research (CIMA), University of Navarra, Pamplona (Spain).

Research Interests

Broad topics driving our current and future research directions.

🧬

Gene Regulatory Networks

Uncovering transcription factor dysregulation in advanced cancers to understand therapy response and identify novel therapeutic targets.

🔎

Single-Cell Transcriptomics

Computational methods for automatic cell-type annotation, in silico perturbation, and regulatory dynamics from scRNA-seq data across disease contexts.

📈

Drug Repurposing

Graph neural network models to predict drug-target interactions and accelerate the translation of ML to clinical practice.

🔍

Interpretable Deep Learning

Autoencoder-based models that translate molecular data into biologically meaningful and interpretable representations.

⚙

Genomic Data Compression

Algorithms and standards for efficient storage and exchange of genomic sequencing data, including contributions to MPEG-G (ISO/IEC).

🏭

Cancer Biology

Applying ML to hematological malignancies, CAR T-cell therapy, and prostate cancer to advance personalized medicine.
